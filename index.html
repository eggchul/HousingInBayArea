Housing in Bay Area

About


Housing has always been an essential concern for people the whole time. Ever since COVID19, most people have to stay home and do work remotely, therefore some of them move out of the city and find a less expensive place to stay. This project aims to analyze the recent housing market in the San Francisco bay area. Some of the cities listed on Niche.com â€œ2021 Best Cities/Suburbs to Live in the San Francisco Bay Area'' were chosen as the target cities for this project. In this project, I then analyze if the number of bedrooms would affect the house value in long term in each of these cities. And then I also check on the monthly percentage change of new inventory in the bay area to seek potential patterns of the market. In addition, Bitcoin has been a hot issue since 2017, especially those dramatic changes in recent months. I bring it together with this analysis and see if there is any connection between Bitcoin and the housing market in the bay area.

Price Trend

Generally, the sales price of a house with more bedrooms will increase faster then a house with less bedrooms. However, there is a turn in 2020. If you navigate with the interactive chart below, you could find that for cities like San Francisco and Foster City, the value of a 5 bedroom house is dropping since 2020.(Zillow)


The above figure(Figure 2) shows the monthly price change of house with different number of bedrooms of the selected city between 2015 and 2020

New Listing


When we check on the data from Relator, we could see that January has been the month that when people will sell their houses. However there is a significant change in 2019 and 2020. The inventory amount of new listing increaed in a sudden around 130% in Jan, 2019 and around 160% in Jan, 2020.

Figure 3 above shows the monthly amount of new listing inventory change in percentage in the bay area.
